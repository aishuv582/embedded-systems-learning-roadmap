Day 23 – Transistor Biasing (DC Biasing of BJT)
🔹 Overview

Today, I learned about biasing in BJT, which is essential to make the transistor work properly as an amplifier by fixing a stable operating point.

🔹 What is Biasing?

Biasing means applying DC voltages to a transistor to set a proper operating point (Q-point).

👉 It ensures the transistor stays in the active region for amplification.

🔹 Why Biasing is Important?
Prevents distortion
Keeps output stable
Maintains proper amplification
Avoids transistor going into cutoff or saturation
🔹 Types of Biasing
1. Fixed Bias
Simple circuit
Uses base resistor
Poor stability
2. Collector-to-Base Bias
Better stability than fixed bias
Feedback from collector improves performance
3. Voltage Divider Bias ⭐ (Most Important)
Most stable method
Uses two resistors (R1, R2)
Widely used in practical circuits
🔹 Q-Point (Operating Point)
The DC operating point of the transistor
Represents values of:
Collector current (Ic)
Collector-Emitter voltage (Vce)

👉 Proper Q-point → No signal distortion

🔹 Stability

Biasing helps to stabilize the circuit against:

Temperature changes
Variations in transistor parameters (β)
🔹 Key Idea

👉 Without proper biasing, amplification will be distorted or unstable

🔹 Applications
Amplifier circuits
Audio systems
Signal processing
🔹 Summary

Biasing is crucial for the proper functioning of a BJT amplifier. Among all methods, voltage divider bias is the most commonly used due to its stability.
