Topic: Pulse Width Modulation (PWM)
What is PWM?

Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) is a technique used to control the average power delivered to a load by varying the width (ON time) of a digital pulse while keeping the frequency constant.

Instead of changing the voltage directly, PWM rapidly switches the signal ON and OFF.

Applications of PWM
DC motor speed control
LED brightness control
Servo motor control
Switching power supplies (SMPS)
Battery charging circuits
Audio amplification (Class-D amplifiers)
Inverters
Advantages
High efficiency (very low power loss)
Precise speed and brightness control
Generates less heat
Easy to generate using microcontrollers
Suitable for digital control systems
Disadvantages
May introduce electrical noise
Requires filtering for analog outputs
High-frequency switching can produce EMI
PWM in Embedded Systems

Most microcontrollers include built-in PWM peripherals.

Examples:
