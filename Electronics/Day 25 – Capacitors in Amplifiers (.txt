Day 24 – Capacitors in Amplifiers (Coupling & Bypass Capacitors)
🔹 Overview

Today, I learned the role of capacitors in amplifier circuits, especially how they are used for coupling and bypass purposes to improve signal quality and performance.

🔹 Why Capacitors are Used in Amplifiers?

Capacitors allow AC signals to pass while blocking DC components.

👉 This helps in:

Maintaining proper biasing
Transferring signals between stages
Improving amplifier efficiency
🔹 Types of Capacitors in Amplifiers
1. Coupling Capacitor
Used to connect two stages of an amplifier
Blocks DC and allows AC signal to pass
Prevents disturbance of biasing between stages

👉 Placed at:

Input (between source and amplifier)
Output (between amplifier and load)
2. Bypass Capacitor
Connected across the emitter resistor
Provides a low-resistance path for AC signals
Improves voltage gain

👉 Without bypass capacitor:

Gain decreases

👉 With bypass capacitor:

Gain increases
🔹 Working Principle
Coupling Capacitor:
Passes AC signal from one stage to another
Blocks DC to maintain independent biasing
Bypass Capacitor:
Shorts AC signals to ground
Removes negative feedback in emitter resistor
Enhances amplification
🔹 Key Concept

👉 Capacitors behave differently for AC and DC:

For DC → Open circuit
For AC → Low impedance (acts like short circuit at high frequency)
🔹 Applications
Multi-stage amplifiers
Audio amplifier circuits
Communication systems
🔹 Summary

Capacitors play a crucial role in amplifier circuits by ensuring smooth signal transfer and improving gain.

Coupling capacitors pass signals between stages
Bypass capacitors increase gain by reducing AC resistance
