Day 46 of Electronics – Analog to Digital Converter (ADC)
ADC converts analog signals into digital data so microcontrollers can process real-world inputs.
Sensors like temperature, pressure, and light sensors usually give analog outputs.
ADC samples the analog voltage at specific intervals and assigns a digital value.
Resolution determines accuracy:
8-bit ADC → 256 levels
10-bit ADC → 1024 levels
12-bit ADC → 4096 levels
Sampling rate indicates how fast the ADC converts signals.
Quantization is the process of mapping analog values to discrete digital levels.
Common ADC types:
Flash ADC
Successive Approximation ADC (SAR)
Dual Slope ADC
SAR ADC is widely used in embedded systems because of good speed and accuracy.
ADC is commonly present inside microcontrollers like STM32, PIC, and AVR.
Applications:
Sensor interfacing
Audio processing
Biomedical devices
Industrial automation
Data acquisition systems
