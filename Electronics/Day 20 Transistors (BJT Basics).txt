Day 20: Transistors (BJT Basics)

Today’s topic is very important because transistors are the heart of electronics—used in amplifiers, switches, and microcontroller circuits.

🔹 What is a Transistor?

A transistor is a semiconductor device used to:

Amplify signals 📈
Act as a switch 🔌

The most common type is BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor).

🔹 Types of BJT
NPN Transistor
PNP Transistor

👉 Most commonly used: NPN

🔹 Structure of BJT

A transistor has 3 terminals:

Emitter (E) → emits charge carriers
Base (B) → controls the transistor
Collector (C) → collects carriers
🔹 Working of NPN Transistor
When base-emitter junction is forward biased
And collector-base is reverse biased

👉 Current flows from Collector → Emitter

Key idea:

👉 Small base current controls large collector current

🔹 Transistor as a Switch

Two states:

OFF State (Cut-off region)
No base current
Transistor OFF
ON State (Saturation region)
Base current present
Transistor ON

👉 This is widely used in:

LEDs
Relays
Embedded systems GPIO control
🔹 Transistor as an Amplifier
Weak signal at base
Strong signal at collector

👉 Used in:

Audio circuits
Signal processing

Base current = 1 mA
Current gain (β) = 100

Then:

Collector current = 100 mA
