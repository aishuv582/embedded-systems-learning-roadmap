Day 36: Crystal Oscillators (Quartz Oscillator)
🔹 What is a Crystal Oscillator?

A crystal oscillator uses a quartz crystal to generate a very stable frequency signal. It is widely used in microcontrollers, clocks, communication systems, etc.

🔹 Why Quartz Crystal?

Quartz has a special property called the Piezoelectric Effect:

👉 When voltage is applied → it vibrates
👉 When it vibrates → it generates voltage

This makes it act like a very precise frequency generator.

🔹 Equivalent Circuit of Crystal

A crystal behaves like an RLC circuit:

Resistance (R)
Inductance (L)
Capacitance (C)

Because of this, it has a very high Q-factor → extremely stable frequency.

🔹 Types of Resonance
1. Series Resonance
Crystal behaves like a low impedance
Frequency is minimum
2. Parallel Resonance
Crystal behaves like a high impedance
Mostly used in circuits

🔹 Advantages

✔ Very high stability
✔ Accurate frequency
✔ Less noise
✔ Used in timing applications (like microcontrollers)

🔹 Disadvantages

✖ Expensive compared to RC oscillators
✖ Limited frequency range
✖ Fragile (physical crystal)

🔹 Applications
Microcontrollers (like 8051 – you’ve used this 👍)
Digital watches
Communication systems
Computers & embedded systems
