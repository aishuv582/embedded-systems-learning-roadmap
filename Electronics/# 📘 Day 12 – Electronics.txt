# 📘 Day 12 – Electronics  
## 🔹 Topic: Capacitance & Capacitors  

---

## 📌 What is Capacitance?

Capacitance is the ability of a system to store electric charge.

- Symbol: **C**  
- Unit: **Farad (F)**  

---

## 📌 What is a Capacitor?

A capacitor is a passive electronic component that stores electrical energy in the form of an electric field.

### 🔧 Construction:
- Two conducting plates  
- One insulating material (dielectric) between them  

---

## 📐 Formula of Capacitance

C = Q / V  

Where:  
- C = Capacitance  
- Q = Charge  
- V = Voltage  

---

## ⚡ Energy Stored in a Capacitor

E = (1/2) C V²  

- Energy is stored in the electric field between the plates  

---

## 🔄 Capacitors in Circuits

### ➤ Capacitors in Series

1 / C_total = 1 / C1 + 1 / C2 + ...  

- Total capacitance decreases  

---

### ➤ Capacitors in Parallel

C_total = C1 + C2 + ...  

- Total capacitance increases  

---

## 🔄 Charging & Discharging

- Charging: Capacitor stores energy when connected to a power source  
- Discharging: Capacitor releases stored energy into the circuit  

---

## 🧠 Key Points

- Capacitors oppose sudden changes in voltage  
- Blocks DC after fully charged  
- Allows AC signals to pass  
- Stores energy in electric field  

---

## 🔌 Types of Capacitors

- Ceramic Capacitor  
- Electrolytic Capacitor  
- Tantalum Capacitor  
- Film Capacitor  

---

## 🚀 Applications

- Power supply filtering  
- Signal coupling and decoupling  
- Timing circuits (RC circuits)  
- Energy storage
