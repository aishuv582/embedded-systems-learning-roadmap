Day 23 of Electronics – BJT as an Amplifier (Detailed Understanding)

BJT using a transistor as an amplifier.

🔹 What is Amplification?

Amplification means:
👉 Increasing the strength of a weak signal

Example:

Input signal → small voltage/current
Output signal → larger voltage/current
🔹 How BJT Works as an Amplifier

A BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor) amplifies when it operates in the:

👉 Active Region

Conditions for Active Region:
Emitter-Base Junction → Forward biased
Collector-Base Junction → Reverse biased
🔹 Common Emitter (CE) Amplifier

This is the most commonly used configuration.

Why?
High voltage gain
High current gain
Moderate power gain
🔹 Basic Circuit Explanation

Components:

Input → Base
Output → Collector
Emitter → Ground
Working:
Small input signal applied at base
Small base current (Ib) flows
This controls large collector current (Ic)

👉 Relationship:

Ic = β × Ib

So,
➡️ Small change in Ib → Large change in Ic → Amplification
