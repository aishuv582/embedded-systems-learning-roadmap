This is an important LC oscillator and very useful for interviews + core understanding.

🔹 What is a Hartley Oscillator?

A Hartley Oscillator is an LC oscillator where the feedback is taken using inductors instead of capacitors.

👉 It uses:

One capacitor (C)
Two inductors (L₁ and L₂) (or a tapped inductor)
🔹 Circuit Idea
Tank circuit = L₁ + L₂ + C
Feedback is taken from the junction of inductors
Amplifier (usually BJT or FET) sustains oscillations

🔹 Working Principle (Simple)
When power is ON, noise starts oscillations
Energy exchanges between inductor and capacitor
Part of output is fed back via inductors
If feedback is positive, oscillations continue
🔹 Condition for Oscillation (Barkhausen)
Loop gain = 1
Total phase shift = 0° or 360°
🔹 Advantages
Simple design
Easy frequency tuning (by changing inductance)
Good for RF applications
🔹 Disadvantages
Frequency stability is less compared to crystal oscillator
Affected by temperature and stray capacitance
🔹 Applications
Radio frequency (RF) oscillators
Signal generators
Tuned circuits
🔹 Hartley vs Colpitts (Quick Insight)
Feature	Hartley	Colpitts
Feedback	Inductors	Capacitors
Stability	Moderate	Better
Tuning	Easy	Slightly complex
