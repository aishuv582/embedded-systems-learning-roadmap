Day 87 of Electronics – Sequence Detector (Overlapping vs Non-Overlapping)

Since you've already covered FSM basics, state minimization, and sequence detectors, today's revision topic is:

Overlapping and Non-Overlapping Sequence Detectors

A sequence detector identifies a specific bit pattern in a serial input stream.

Example Sequence: 1011
1. Non-Overlapping Sequence Detector

After detecting the sequence, the FSM resets and starts searching for a new sequence from the beginning.

Input: 1011011

Detection:

First 1011 → Detected
FSM resets
Remaining bits are checked as a new sequence

Advantage:

Simpler design
Fewer false detections
2. Overlapping Sequence Detector

After detection, the FSM reuses bits that may be part of the next sequence.

Input: 1011011

Detection:

First 1011 → Detected
Last bits can be reused
Second 1011 can also be detected

Advantage:

Detects sequences faster
More efficient for continuous data streams
Comparison
Feature	Non-Overlapping	Overlapping
Reuses bits after detection	No	Yes
Number of detections	Fewer	More
Complexity	Lower	Higher
Efficiency	Lower	Higher
