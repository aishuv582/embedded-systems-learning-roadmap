Day 72 of Electronics – Johnson Counter (Twisted Ring Counter)

After learning Ring Counters, the next important shift-register-based counter is the Johnson Counter.

What is a Johnson Counter?

A Johnson Counter is a shift register counter in which the inverted output of the last flip-flop is fed back to the input of the first flip-flop.

Unlike a Ring Counter, a Johnson Counter produces 2N states using N flip-flops.

For example:

4 flip-flops → 8 states
5 flip-flops → 10 states
Working of a 4-Bit Johnson Counter

State sequence:

Clock Pulse	Q3 Q2 Q1 Q0
Initial	0000
1	1000
2	1100
3	1110
4	1111
5	0111
6	0011
7	0001
8	0000

The sequence then repeats.

Advantages

✅ More states than a Ring Counter for the same number of flip-flops

✅ Simple decoding logic

✅ Easy implementation using D flip-flops

✅ Widely used in digital sequencing circuits

Ring Counter vs Johnson Counter
Feature	Ring Counter	Johnson Counter
Feedback	Direct output fed back	Inverted output fed back
States	N	2N
Efficiency	Lower	Higher
Decoding	Simple	Simple
Applications
Timing sequence generation
Frequency division
Digital control circuits
State machines
LED chasers and pattern generators
