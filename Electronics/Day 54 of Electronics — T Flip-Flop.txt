Day 54 of Electronics — T Flip-Flop
What is a T Flip-Flop?

A T Flip-Flop (Toggle Flip-Flop) is a flip-flop that changes its output state whenever the input is HIGH during the clock pulse.

T = Toggle
It is mainly used in counters and frequency division circuits.
Inputs and Outputs
Input
T → Toggle input
CLK → Clock signal
Outputs
Q → Normal output
Q̅ → Complement output
Working Principle
Case 1: T = 0

No change occurs.

Present state = Next state
Case 2: T = 1

Output toggles.

0 becomes 1
1 becomes 0

Construction of T Flip-Flop

A T flip-flop can be made using:

JK Flip-Flop by connecting:

Applications
Binary counters
Frequency divider circuits
Digital clocks
Sequential circuits
Advantages
Simple design
Useful for counting operations
Efficient toggle operation
Disadvantages
Not suitable for direct data storage
Continuous toggling may create propagation delay in ripple counters
Important Interview Points
T flip-flop toggles when T = 1.
It is derived from JK flip-flop.
Mostly used in counters.
Each toggle divides frequency by 2.
