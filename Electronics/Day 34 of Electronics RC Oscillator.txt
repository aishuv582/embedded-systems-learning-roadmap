Day 34 of Electronics: RC Oscillators (Phase Shift & Wien Bridge)

You’ve already covered oscillator basics—today let’s go one level deeper into RC oscillators, which are widely used for generating low-frequency signals (audio range).

🔹 What is an RC Oscillator?

An RC oscillator uses resistors (R) and capacitors (C) along with an amplifier (like BJT or Op-Amp) to generate a stable sine wave output.

👉 No inductors here—just R and C components!

🔸 1. RC Phase Shift Oscillator
💡 Concept:
Uses 3 RC sections to produce 180° phase shift
Amplifier (like transistor) gives another 180°
Total = 360° (or 0°) → satisfies oscillation condition
