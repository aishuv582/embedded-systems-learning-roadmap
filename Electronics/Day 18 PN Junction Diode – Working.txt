Day 17: PN Junction Diode – Working & V-I Characteristics
🔹 1. What is a PN Junction Diode?

A PN Junction Diode is formed by joining:

P-type semiconductor (holes are majority carriers)
N-type semiconductor (electrons are majority carriers)

👉 It is the most basic and important semiconductor device.

🔹 2. Formation of Depletion Region

When P and N materials are joined:

Electrons move from N → P
Holes move from P → N

This creates a region with:

No free charge carriers
Fixed ions

👉 This region is called the Depletion Region

🔹 3. Barrier Potential (Voltage)

Due to charge separation:

An electric field is formed
It opposes further movement of charges

👉 This voltage is called Barrier Voltage

Typical values:

Silicon: 0.7 V
Germanium: 0.3 V
🔹 4. Biasing of Diode
🔸 Forward Bias
P → Positive terminal
N → Negative terminal

👉 Effect:

Depletion layer reduces
Current flows
🔸 Reverse Bias
P → Negative terminal
N → Positive terminal

👉 Effect:

Depletion layer increases
Current almost zero
🔹 5. V-I Characteristics of Diode

This shows the relationship between:

Voltage (V)
Current (I)
🔸 Forward Region:
No current until threshold voltage
After threshold → current increases rapidly
🔸 Reverse Region:
Very small current (leakage current)
At high voltage → breakdown occurs
