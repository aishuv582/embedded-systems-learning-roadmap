Day 26: Multistage Amplifiers
🔹 What is a Multistage Amplifier?

A multistage amplifier is formed by:

👉 Connecting two or more amplifiers in series

Output of one stage → input of next stage
Used when a single amplifier cannot provide enough gain
🔹 Why Do We Need It?

A single amplifier:

Has limited gain
Cannot drive heavy loads effectively

👉 So we combine stages to:

Increase overall gain
Improve signal strength
Drive output devices (like speakers)
🔹 Types of Multistage Coupling
1. RC Coupling (Most Common)
Uses resistor + capacitor
Best for audio frequencies

✅ Advantages:

Simple
Low cost
Good frequency response

❌ Disadvantage:

Power loss
2. Transformer Coupling
Uses a transformer

✅ Advantages:

High efficiency
Good impedance matching

❌ Disadvantages:

Expensive
Bulky
3. Direct Coupling
No capacitor or transformer
Stages directly connected

✅ Advantages:

Works for low frequency (DC signals)

❌ Disadvantage:

Temperature variations affect performance
