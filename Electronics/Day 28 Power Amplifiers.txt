Day 27: Power Amplifiers
🔹 What is a Power Amplifier?

A power amplifier is designed to:

👉 Increase the power of a signal to drive loads like speakers

Unlike small-signal amplifiers:

Focus is on power (not just voltage gain)
Used at the output stage
🔹 Why Do We Need Power Amplifiers?
Signals from previous stages are weak
Cannot drive devices like:
Speakers
Motors
Output loads

👉 Power amplifier boosts signal strength to usable level

🔹 Types of Power Amplifiers (Classes)
🔸 Class A Amplifier
Transistor conducts for full 360°

✅ Advantages:

High signal quality
No distortion

❌ Disadvantages:

Very low efficiency (~25–30%)
Generates heat
🔸 Class B Amplifier
Conducts for 180°

✅ Advantages:

Better efficiency (~70%)

❌ Disadvantage:

Crossover distortion
🔸 Class AB Amplifier
Conducts for between 180° and 360°

✅ Advantages:

Combines benefits of A & B
Less distortion + good efficiency

👉 Most commonly used in real circuits

🔸 Class C Amplifier
Conducts for less than 180°

✅ Advantages:

Very high efficiency

❌ Disadvantage:

High distortion

👉 Used in RF applications

🔹 Real-Life Applications
Audio systems 🔊

👉 Think like this:

Small amplifier → “signal clarity”
