Day 40 of Electronics – Frequency Stability in Oscillators

Yesterday you covered oscillators, so today we’ll go one level deeper: how stable the frequency is (very important in real embedded systems).

🔹 What is Frequency Stability?

It means how constant the oscillator frequency remains over time.

👉 Ideally: frequency should be constant
👉 Practically: it varies due to different factors

🔹 Types of Frequency Variations
Short-term stability
Small fluctuations over milliseconds/seconds
Caused by noise
Long-term stability
Drift over minutes/hours/days
Caused by temperature, aging
🔹 Factors Affecting Stability
Temperature
Components (R, L, C) change with temperature
Leads to frequency drift
Power Supply Variations
Voltage changes → frequency changes
Component Aging
Over time, components degrade
Noise
External interference affects signal
🔹 Stability Comparison of Oscillators
Oscillator Type	Stability
RC Oscillator	Low ❌
LC Oscillator	Medium ⚠️
Crystal Oscillator	High ✅

👉 Crystal oscillators are best because they use quartz crystal vibration.

🔹 Why Crystal Oscillators are Stable?
Based on mechanical resonance
Very high Q factor (Quality factor)
Minimal frequency variation
🔹 Frequency of Crystal Oscillator

The frequency is determined by crystal properties (not by R, L, C like others).
