Day 25: Frequency Response of Amplifiers

This topic directly builds on yesterday (capacitors), and it’s very important for interviews + real circuit design.

🔹 1. What is Frequency Response?

👉 Frequency response tells how an amplifier’s gain changes with frequency.

Not all signals are amplified equally — it depends on frequency.

🔸 2. Frequency Regions

An amplifier works in 3 regions:

🟢 1. Low-Frequency Region
Gain decreases
Due to:
Coupling capacitors
Bypass capacitor

👉 At low frequency:

Capacitor reactance is high
Signal gets blocked
🟡 2. Mid-Frequency Region
Gain is maximum and constant
Capacitors act like short circuits
This is the ideal working region
🔴 3. High-Frequency Region
Gain decreases again
Due to:
Internal transistor capacitances
Wiring/parasitic effects
🔹 3. Frequency Response Curve

It looks like a band-pass shape:

y=Gain(f)

👉 Shape:

Low freq → Gain rises
Mid freq → Flat
High freq → Falls
🔸 4. Important Terms
✅ Cut-off Frequency (f₁ and f₂)
Lower cut-off frequency (f₁) → Gain drops at low freq
Upper cut-off frequency (f₂) → Gain drops at high freq

👉 At cut-off:

Gain becomes 70.7% of maximum (or -3 dB)

🔹 5. Why Gain Drops?
🔽 Low Frequency:
Capacitors block signal
🔽 High Frequency:
Internal capacitances create leakage paths
Signal gets weakened
🔸 6. Practical Importance
Audio amplifiers → Need wide bandwidth (20 Hz – 20 kHz)
Communication systems → Need specific frequency range
Embedded systems → Sensor signal conditioning
