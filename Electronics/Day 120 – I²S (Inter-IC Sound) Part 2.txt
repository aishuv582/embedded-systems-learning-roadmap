Day 120 – I²S (Inter-IC Sound) Protocol
Objective

Understand the I²S (Inter-IC Sound) protocol, which is widely used to transfer digital audio between integrated circuits such as microcontrollers, codecs, DACs, ADCs, and audio amplifiers.

What is I²S?

I²S (Inter-IC Sound) is a synchronous serial communication protocol designed specifically for transmitting digital audio data.

It was introduced by Philips (now NXP) and is commonly used in:

Audio codecs
Digital microphones
DACs and ADCs
Bluetooth audio modules
MP3 players
Embedded multimedia systems
Why Not UART, SPI, or I²C?

General communication protocols are not optimized for continuous audio streaming.

I²S provides:

Continuous high-speed audio transfer
Precise synchronization
Separate clock lines for accurate sampling
Support for stereo audio
I²S Signal Lines
1. Bit Clock (BCLK/SCK)
Synchronizes the transmission of each data bit.
One pulse per transmitted bit.
2. Word Select (WS/LRCLK)
Indicates whether the transmitted data belongs to the Left or Right audio channel.
Low = Left channel
High = Right channel (common convention)
3. Serial Data (SD)
Carries the actual audio sample bits.
Optional
Master Clock (MCLK)
Used by some audio codecs.
Usually 256× or 384× the sampling frequency.
Working Principle

Example:

Sampling Rate = 44.1 kHz
Resolution = 16 bits
Stereo

The transmitter sends:

Left Sample → Right Sample → Left Sample → Right Sample...

The WS signal changes state after each audio word.

Data Format
WS : 0 0 0 0 | 1 1 1 1

SD :
Left Audio Bits
1010110010101010

Right Audio Bits
1101001110010101

Master and Slave
Master

Generates:

Bit Clock
Word Select
Master Clock (optional)
Slave
Uses the clocks generated by the master.
Sends or receives audio data.
Advantages
High-quality digital audio transmission
Low jitter
Supports stereo audio
Simple interface
Widely supported by microcontrollers and codecs
Limitations
Designed only for audio communication
Requires multiple clock lines
Short-distance communication (typically on the same PCB)
Applications
Smart speakers
Bluetooth headphones
Voice assistants
Digital microphones
Audio recorders
Automotive infotainment systems
Embedded multimedia devices
