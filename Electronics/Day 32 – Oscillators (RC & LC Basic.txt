Day 32 – Oscillators (RC & LC Basics)
🔹 What is an Oscillator?

An oscillator is a circuit that generates a continuous waveform (without external input signal).

👉 Output types:

Sine wave
Square wave
Triangle wave

👉 Used in:

Microcontrollers (clock signal)
Communication systems
Signal generators
🔹 Basic Principle (Very Important)

An oscillator works using:

👉 Positive Feedback + Amplifier

Condition for oscillation:

Aβ=1

Where:

A = Gain of amplifier
β = Feedback factor

✔ This is called Barkhausen Criterion

🔹 Types of Oscillators
1. RC Oscillator

Used for low frequency signals

👉 Example:

Phase Shift Oscillator
Wien Bridge Oscillator

✔ Uses resistors + capacitors
✔ Simple design

2. LC Oscillator

Used for high frequency signals

👉 Example:

Hartley Oscillator
Colpitts Oscillator

✔ Uses inductor + capacitor
✔ Used in RF applications
