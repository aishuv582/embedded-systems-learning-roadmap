Day 28: Feedback in Amplifiers (Negative Feedback)
🔹 What is Feedback?

Feedback means taking a portion of the output signal and feeding it back to the input.

There are two types:

Positive Feedback → Increases gain (can cause instability)
Negative Feedback → Reduces gain but improves performance ✅
🔹 Negative Feedback Concept

In most amplifier circuits, we use negative feedback because it makes the system stable and reliable.

🔹 Advantages of Negative Feedback
✅ 1. Stabilizes Gain

Even if components change, gain remains nearly constant.

✅ 2. Reduces Distortion

Improves signal quality.

✅ 3. Increases Bandwidth

Amplifier works over a wider frequency range.

✅ 4. Reduces Noise

Cleaner output signal.

🔹 Disadvantages
Gain is reduced
Circuit becomes slightly complex
🔹 Types of Negative Feedback
Voltage Series Feedback
Voltage Shunt Feedback
Current Series Feedback
Current Shunt Feedback

👉 These types depend on:

What is sampled (voltage/current)
How it is fed back (series/shunt)
🔹 Real-Life Example
Audio amplifiers → clear sound without distortion 🎧
Operational amplifiers (Op-Amps) → precise control
Embedded systems circuits → stable sensor readings
🔹 Quick Summary
Feedback = Output → Input
Negative feedback = Stability + Better performance
Formula: Gain reduces but quality improves
