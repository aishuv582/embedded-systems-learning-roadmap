Day 37 of Electronics: Crystal Oscillators
🔹 Definition

A crystal oscillator is an electronic circuit that uses a quartz crystal to produce a precise and stable frequency signal.

🔹 Key Concept

It works based on the 👉 Piezoelectric Effect

Apply voltage → crystal vibrates
Crystal vibrates → generates voltage
This creates continuous oscillations
🔹 Equivalent Model

A crystal behaves like an RLC circuit:

Resistance (R)
Inductance (L)
Capacitance (C)

👉 This gives it a very high Q-factor (sharp and stable frequency)

🔹 Types
Series Resonance
Low impedance
Frequency slightly lower
Parallel Resonance
High impedance
Most commonly used
🔹 Working
Amplifier provides gain
Crystal selects exact frequency
Feedback sustains oscillation

👉 Output: Highly stable sinusoidal signal

🔹 Advantages

✔ Very high frequency stability
✔ High accuracy
✔ Low noise

🔹 Applications
Microcontrollers (clock signal)
Digital watches
Communication systems
Embedded systems (very important for your goal 🚀)
