Day 68 of Electronics – Modulo Counters (MOD Counters)

Since you've already covered ripple counters and synchronous counters, the next important topic is Modulo Counters.

What is a Modulo Counter?

A modulo (MOD) counter counts a fixed number of states and then resets to zero.

Formula:

MOD Number=Number of states before repeating

Examples:

MOD-4 Counter → Counts 0,1,2,3 and repeats.
MOD-10 Counter → Counts 0 to 9 and repeats.
MOD-16 Counter → Counts 0 to 15 and repeats.
Why are MOD Counters Needed?

Many real-world applications require counting a specific number of events.

Examples:

Digital clocks (MOD-60 for seconds)
Frequency dividers
Traffic light controllers
Event counters
Microcontroller timers

MOD-N Counter

A counter that counts from 0 to N−1 and then repeats.

Examples:

Counter	States
MOD-2	2
MOD-4	4
MOD-8	8
MOD-10	10
MOD-12	12
MOD-60	60
