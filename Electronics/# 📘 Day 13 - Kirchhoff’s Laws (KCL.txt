# 📘 Day 13 - Kirchhoff’s Laws (KCL & KVL)

## 🔹 Overview
Today I revised **Kirchhoff’s Laws**, which are essential for analyzing electrical circuits.

---

## 🔹 1. Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL)

**Definition:**  
The total current entering a node is equal to the total current leaving the node.

**Formula:**
Σ I(in) = Σ I(out)

**Key Point:**  
- Based on **conservation of charge**
- Current cannot accumulate at a node

**Example:**  
If 3A and 2A enter a node, then 5A must leave.

---

## 🔹 2. Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL)

**Definition:**  
The sum of all voltages in a closed loop is equal to zero.

**Formula:**
Σ V = 0

**Key Point:**  
- Based on **conservation of energy**
- Total voltage supplied = total voltage drop

**Example:**  
10V - 4V - 6V = 0 ✔️

---

## 🔹 3. Applications

- Circuit analysis
- Node voltage method
- Loop current method
- Embedded systems hardware design

---

## 🔹 4. Key Differences

| KCL | KVL |
|-----|-----|
| Works at node | Works in loop |
| Based on charge | Based on energy |
| Deals with current | Deals with voltage |

---

## 🔹 5. Practice Question

A node has:
- Incoming current = 4A + 6A
- Outgoing current = 5A + x

Find x.

**Solution:**  
Total incoming = 10A  
10 = 5 + x  
x = 5A

---

## 🚀 Conclusion

Kirchhoff’s Laws are fundamental tools for solving complex circuits and are widely used in electronics and embedded systems.
