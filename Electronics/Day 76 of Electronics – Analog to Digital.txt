Day 76 of Electronics – Analog to Digital Converter (ADC) Architectures

You may have learned what an ADC does. Today focus on the different ADC architectures, which are frequently asked in interviews.

Why ADC is Needed

Most real-world signals (temperature, pressure, sound, light) are analog, while microcontrollers process digital data.

ADC converts:

Analog Signal → Digital Value

Types of ADCs
1. Flash ADC
Fastest ADC type.
Uses many comparators.
Conversion happens in a single step.

Advantages

Extremely high speed.

Disadvantages

High power consumption.
Expensive for high resolutions.

Applications

Oscilloscopes
Radar systems
High-speed communication
2. Successive Approximation Register (SAR) ADC
Most commonly used ADC in microcontrollers.
Performs a binary search to find the digital value.

Advantages

Good balance of speed, accuracy, and power.

Applications

Embedded systems
Sensor interfacing
Data acquisition systems
3. Dual Slope ADC
Integrates the input signal over time.
Highly accurate but slow.

Applications

Digital multimeters
Precision measurement instruments
4. Sigma-Delta ADC
Uses oversampling and digital filtering.

Advantages

Very high resolution.
Excellent noise performance.

Applications

Audio systems
Precision sensors
Industrial instrumentation
Interview Questions

Q1. Which ADC is commonly found in microcontrollers?

SAR ADC.

Q2. Which ADC is the fastest?

Flash ADC.

Q3. Which ADC provides the highest accuracy for measurement instruments?

Dual Slope ADC.

Q4. Which ADC is preferred for audio applications?

Sigma-Delta ADC.
Quick Comparison
ADC Type	Speed	Accuracy	Cost

Dual Slope	Low	Very High	Medium
