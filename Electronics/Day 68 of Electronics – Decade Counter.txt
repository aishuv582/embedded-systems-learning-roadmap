Day 66 of Electronics – Decade Counter (MOD-10 Counter)

After learning synchronous counters and MOD-N counters, the next important topic is the Decade Counter.

What is a Decade Counter?

A decade counter is a counter that counts from 0 to 9 (10 states) and then resets back to 0.

It is also called a MOD-10 Counter because it has 10 valid states.

Counting Sequence
Decimal	Binary
0	0000
1	0001
2	0010
3	0011
4	0100
5	0101
6	0110
7	0111
8	1000
9	1001
Reset	0000

Working Principle

A normal 4-bit binary counter counts from 0 to 15.

A decade counter detects the count 1010 (decimal 10) and immediately resets all flip-flops to 0000.

Thus, only the states 0–9 are used.

Reset Condition

When:

Q3 Q2 Q1 Q0 = 1010

the reset circuit activates and clears the counter.

Advantages

✅ Counts decimal digits directly

✅ Easy interface with displays

✅ Widely used in digital clocks

✅ Simple implementation

Disadvantages

❌ Additional reset logic required

❌ Some designs may have glitches due to reset timing

Applications
Digital clocks
Frequency counters
Digital stopwatches
Calculator circuits
Seven-segment display systems
Event counting systems
