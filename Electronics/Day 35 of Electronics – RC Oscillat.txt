Day 34 of Electronics – RC Oscillators

Since you’ve already started oscillators, today let’s go deeper into one of the most important types used in embedded systems and analog circuits: RC Oscillators.

🔹 What is an RC Oscillator?

An RC Oscillator uses Resistors (R) and Capacitors (C) to generate a sinusoidal waveform without any external input signal.

👉 It works based on:

Charging and discharging of capacitors
Positive feedback
Phase shift
🔹 Types of RC Oscillators
1. RC Phase Shift Oscillator
Uses 3 RC stages to produce a total 180° phase shift
Amplifier (like BJT/Op-Amp) adds another 180°
Total = 360° (sustained oscillation)

🔹 Working Principle (Simple Understanding)
Capacitor starts charging
Voltage builds up across RC network
Feedback is given to amplifier
Output keeps repeating → Oscillation
🔹 Conditions for Oscillation (Important Interview Point)

This is based on Barkhausen Criterion:

✔ Loop gain = 1
✔ Total phase shift = 360°

🔹 Applications
Audio signal generation
Function generators
Clock circuits (low frequency)
Testing circuits
