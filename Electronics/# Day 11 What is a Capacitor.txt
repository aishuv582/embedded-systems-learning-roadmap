What is a Capacitor?

A capacitor is an electronic component that stores electrical energy in the form of an electric field.

It consists of:

Two conductive plates
A dielectric (insulating material) between them
🧮 Formula
C = Q / V

Where:

C = Capacitance (Farad)
Q = Charge
V = Voltage
📏 Units
Farad (F)
Common Values:
Microfarad (µF)
Nanofarad (nF)
Picofarad (pF)
🔍 Types of Capacitors
Ceramic Capacitor
Electrolytic Capacitor (Polarized)
Tantalum Capacitor
Film Capacitor
🔄 Working Principle
🔸 Charging
Stores electrical energy when connected to a voltage source
Voltage increases gradually
🔸 Discharging
Releases stored energy when disconnected from the source
💡 Key Concepts
Blocks DC after fully charged
Allows AC signals to pass
Used for energy storage and filtering
🛠️ Applications
Power supply filtering
Timing circuits
Signal coupling and decoupling
Used in chargers and electronic devices
✅ Conclusion

Capacitors play a crucial role in electronic circuits by storing energy, filtering signals, and stabilizing voltage.
