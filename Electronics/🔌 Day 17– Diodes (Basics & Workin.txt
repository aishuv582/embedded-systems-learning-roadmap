🔌 Day 16 – Diodes (Basics & Working)
📌 What is a Diode?

A diode is a semiconductor device that allows current to flow in only one direction.

👉 Think of it like a one-way valve for electric current.

⚙️ Structure of a Diode

A diode is made of:

P-type material (positive side → Anode)
N-type material (negative side → Cathode)

When joined, they form a PN junction.

🔄 Working of a Diode
1️⃣ Forward Bias (ON condition)
Anode → connected to positive
Cathode → connected to negative

✅ Current flows
✅ Diode conducts

👉 Voltage required:

Silicon diode → ~0.7V
Germanium diode → ~0.3V
2️⃣ Reverse Bias (OFF condition)
Anode → negative
Cathode → positive

❌ No current flows
❌ Diode blocks current

📈 Diode Symbol
   Anode     Cathode
     |>| 

➡️ Arrow shows direction of current flow

💡 Key Concept: Barrier Voltage
Small voltage needed to start conduction
Important in real circuits (not ideal diode)
🔧 Applications of Diodes
Rectifiers (AC → DC conversion)
Protection circuits
Signal clipping & clamping
LED (light emitting diode)
🧠 Quick Revision
Diode = unidirectional device
Forward bias = conducts
Reverse bias = blocks
Used in power supplies & embedded circuits
