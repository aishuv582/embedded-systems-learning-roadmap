Day 46 of Electronics – Digital to Analog Converter (DAC)

Today’s topic: Digital to Analog Converter (DAC) 🎛️

What is a DAC?

A DAC converts digital binary data into an equivalent analog voltage or current signal.

It is the opposite of an ADC.

Why DAC is Important?

Processors and microcontrollers work with digital data, but many real-world devices require analog signals. DAC helps digital systems interact with analog devices.

Working Principle
Digital binary input is given.
DAC converts each binary value into proportional voltage/current.
Analog output signal is produced.
Common Types of DAC
Binary Weighted Resistor DAC
Uses resistors weighted according to binary values.
Simple but difficult for high resolutions.
R-2R Ladder DAC
Uses only two resistor values (R and 2R).
Most widely used DAC architecture.
Important Terms
Resolution
Smallest analog change produced by 1-bit change.
Higher bits → better accuracy.
Settling Time
Time required for output to reach final value.
Accuracy
Closeness of output to expected analog value.
Applications
Audio systems and speakers
Signal generators
Motor control
Embedded systems
Communication systems
Video processing
Advantages
Converts digital data into usable analog signals
Essential for multimedia and control systems
Disadvantages
Output may contain noise
High-resolution DACs are costly
