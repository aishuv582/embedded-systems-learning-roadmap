# 📘 Day 5: Series and Parallel Circuits

## 🔹 Overview

Today I learned about **Series and Parallel Circuits**, which are fundamental concepts in electronics used to understand how components are connected in a circuit.

---

## 🔸 1. Series Circuit

### 👉 Definition:

A series circuit is a circuit in which components are connected one after another in a single path.

### 🔹 Key Characteristics:

* Current is the **same** through all components
* Voltage is **divided** across components

### 🔹 Formulas:

* Current:
  I = I₁ = I₂ = I₃

* Voltage:
  V = V₁ + V₂ + V₃

* Total Resistance:
  R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃

---

## 🔸 2. Parallel Circuit

### 👉 Definition:

A parallel circuit is a circuit where components are connected across the same voltage source, providing multiple paths for current.

### 🔹 Key Characteristics:

* Voltage is the **same** across all branches
* Current is **divided** among branches

### 🔹 Formulas:

* Voltage:
  V = V₁ = V₂ = V₃

* Current:
  I = I₁ + I₂ + I₃

* Total Resistance:
  1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃

---

## 🔸 Comparison Table

| Feature    | Series Circuit | Parallel Circuit |
| ---------- | -------------- | ---------------- |
| Path       | Single         | Multiple         |
| Current    | Same           | Divided          |
| Voltage    | Divided        | Same             |
| Resistance | Increases      | Decreases        |

---

## 🔸 Real-Life Examples

* Series Circuit: Decorative lights
* Parallel Circuit: Household wiring

---



## 🔹 Conclusion

Understanding series and parallel circuits helps in analyzing and designing electrical systems. These concepts are essential for embedded systems and hardware development.
