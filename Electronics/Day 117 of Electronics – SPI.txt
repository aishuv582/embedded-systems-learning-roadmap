Day 115 of Electronics – SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface)
Objective

Understand the SPI communication protocol, its architecture, working principle, signals, advantages, disadvantages, and applications.

1. What is SPI?

SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface) is a synchronous serial communication protocol developed by Motorola. It is used for high-speed communication between a master device (usually a microcontroller) and one or more slave devices.

Unlike UART, SPI does not require start or stop bits, making it much faster.

Examples:

Microcontroller ↔ EEPROM
Microcontroller ↔ SD Card
Microcontroller ↔ ADC/DAC
Microcontroller ↔ TFT Display
Microcontroller ↔ Sensors
2. SPI Architecture

SPI consists of one Master and one or more Slave devices.

           Master
         +-----------+
MOSI ---->|           |
MISO <----|           |
SCLK ---->|           |
SS   ---->|           |
         +-----------+

               |

       ---------------------

      Slave 1      Slave 2

The master generates the clock and selects the slave.

3. SPI Signals

SPI uses 4 communication lines.

MOSI (Master Out Slave In)
Carries data from Master to Slave.

Example:

Master -------> Slave
MISO (Master In Slave Out)
Carries data from Slave to Master.
Slave --------> Master
SCLK (Serial Clock)

Generated only by the Master.

Synchronizes data transmission.

SS / CS (Slave Select / Chip Select)

Used to select which slave should communicate.

Active Low.

SS = 0

Slave Selected
SS = 1

Slave Disabled
4. Working Principle

Step 1

Master selects slave.

SS = LOW

↓

Step 2

Master starts clock.

SCLK

Step 3

Master sends data through MOSI.

↓

Step 4

Slave simultaneously sends data through MISO.

↓

Step 5

After communication,

SS = HIGH

Communication ends.

5. Full Duplex Communication

SPI supports Full Duplex communication.

That means:

Master ---> Slave

AND

Slave ---> Master

Both happen simultaneously.

This makes SPI much faster than protocols like I²C.

6. SPI Data Transfer

Suppose Master sends:

10110010

Slave sends:

01001101

Clock:

↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑

After 8 clock pulses,

Master receives Slave data.

Slave receives Master data.
